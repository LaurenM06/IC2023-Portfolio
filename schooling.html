Schooling

Perry Meridian High School

Currently, I am attending Perry Meridian High school. This school provides me with a wide range of opportunities, such as clubs and different graduation pathways. This school is incredibly diverse, allowing me to interact with a lot of different people and cultures, and preparing me for the real world. Not only are the extracurriculars at this school numerous, but they each add a different perspective to my academic life, as well as allow me to gain networking and other usable life skills. The teachers at this school are incredibly helpful and there is always at least one teacher that is willing to assist me if I have any questions about any given topic. I make it a point to put my academics first, maintaining above a 4.2 GPA, and making sure to challenge myself in my classes.

Early College

At Perry Meridian High School there is an optional Early College pathway that allows students to take dual credit courses and secure college credits to prepare them for college. In this program there is an option to take enough dual-credit courses to award you an associates degree, which is the track I am currently taking. This is incredibly useful to those planning on obtaining a bachelor's degree, as many of the credits are applicable to general education classes a majority of majors will require you to take your freshman year. This is an incredible opportunity to save money, challenge yourself, and possibly save time in college by applying those credits.

AP Courses

Perry Meridian High School has an assortment of AP classes available to its students, as I am taking a majority of dual credit courses, there isn’t much room in my schedule for AP courses. However, I do try to challenge myself when it comes to academics, so I am taking AP courses alongside the dual-credit track. Through my high school career, I will have taken 4 AP classes, focused in STEM. These classes include: AP Calculus AB, AP Calculus BC, AP Physics1, and AP Computer Science.
